## 1.1 Laptop Hardware

### Storage
- **Li-ion** — Lithium Ion battery
- **LiPo** — Lithium Ion Polymer battery
- **SO-DIMM** — Small Outline Dual In-line Memory Module (laptop RAM)
- **Magnetic Disk** — 2.5 inch form factor for laptops, 3.5 inch for desktops
- **SSD** — 2.5 inch form factor, 2 connectors (1 for data, 1 for power)
- **M.2** — smaller form factor SSD, slots directly into motherboard

### Migration
- **Imaging / Cloning Software** — used to migrate data from one drive to another
  - Example: *Macrium Reflect*

### Wireless
- **802.11** — Wireless LAN (Wi-Fi)
- **Bluetooth** — Personal Area Network (PAN)
- **Mini PCI** — older wireless card standard (2 wires)
- **Mini PCI Express** — newer wireless card standard
- **NFC** — Near Field Communication

## 1.2 Connecting Mobile Devices

### USB Standards
- **Micro B** — older Android phone charger (the small trapezoid shaped one)
- **Mini B** — older standard, less common now
- **Type A** — the standard rectangular USB plug
- **USB-C** — 24 pin, double sided connector
  - Can transmit: DisplayPort, HDMI, Thunderbolt, power, data
- **Lightning** — Apple proprietary connector, 8 pin digital signals

### Wireless Connections
- **NFC** — transfers small amounts of data wirelessly over a very short range

### Tethering vs Hotspot
- **Hotspot** — shares internet connection with multiple devices
- **Tethering** — same as hotspot but only for 1 device

### Accessories
- **Stylus** — advanced writing tool, like a smart pen
  - Example: *Apple Pencil for iPad*
- **Headsets** — connects via 3.5mm TRRS connector (Tip Ring Ring Sleeve)
- **Docking Station** — expands laptop ports when sitting at a desk
- **Port Replicator** — similar to docking station, replicates ports
- **Drawing Pad** — external digitizer, works like a stylus on a surface

## TryHackMe — Introduction to Cyber Security

### Offensive Security
- Thinking like an attacker to find weaknesses **before** real hackers do
- Uses tools like `dirb` to find hidden pages on websites
- Lines starting with `+` in dirb output = pages found
- Goal: find and report vulnerabilities so they can be fixed

### Defensive Security
- Process of defending and securing devices and systems
- **Step 1** — Spot activity that doesn't look normal (stored as **alerts**)
- **Step 2** — Understand what kind of attack it is
- **Step 3** — Take steps to stop the attack

### Career Paths in Cybersecurity

| Role | Description |
|---|---|
| **Security Analyst** | Monitors, investigates and responds to activity on networks and devices. Digital defenders of an organisation |
| **Security Engineer** | Builds and maintains systems that protect an organisation. Architects of cyber security |
| **Penetration Tester** | Safely tries to break into systems to check how secure they are. Also called ethical hacking or pentesting |
